üíº Fundamental Analysis: The Pillars of the Rally

1. Bitcoin ETFs: The Game Has Changed

Bitcoin spot ETFs, approved in January 2024, continue to be the main catalyst for the rally:

  • Cumulative net inflow: Over $80 billion in 2025
  • BlackRock iShares Bitcoin Trust (IBIT): Largest ETF with $35 billion in AUM
  • Fidelity Wise Origin Bitcoin Fund: $22 billion in AUM
  • Grayscale Bitcoin Trust (GBTC): Converted to ETF, $18 billion

:::info ETF Impact Institutional demand via ETFs is buying more Bitcoin than is mined daily, creating a supply deficit that pushes the price upward. :::

2. 2024 Halving: Effects Still Ongoing

Bitcoin's fourth halving occurred in April 2024, reducing miner rewards from 6.25 BTC to 3.125 BTC per block.

Historical Halving Pattern:

  • 2012: 9,000% increase in the following 18 months
  • 2016: 2,900% increase in the following 18 months
  • 2020: 700% increase in the following 18 months
  • 2024: We're in the 19th month post-halving

:::warning Attention Historically, the biggest gains occur between 12-18 months after the halving. We're exactly in that time window! :::

3. Growing Institutional Adoption

Bitcoin's institutional adoption continues to accelerate in 2025:

Companies with Bitcoin on Balance Sheet:

  • MicroStrategy: 250,000 BTC (~$25 billion)
  • Tesla: 48,000 BTC (~$5 billion)
  • Block (Square): 12,000 BTC
  • Marathon Digital: 25,000 BTC

Countries with Bitcoin Reserves:

  • El Salvador: 5,800 BTC
  • Bhutan: 13,000 BTC (hydroelectric mining)
  • Rumors: Other countries considering strategic reserves

4. Macroeconomic Context

The 2025 macroeconomic environment is favorable for risk assets:

  • Controlled inflation: Between 2-3% in the US
  • Stable interest rates: Fed maintains rate between 4.5-5%
  • Weakened dollar: Benefits alternative assets
  • Geopolitical tensions: Bitcoin as a hedge
