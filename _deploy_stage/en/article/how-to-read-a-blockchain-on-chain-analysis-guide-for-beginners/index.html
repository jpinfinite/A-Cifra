How to Read the Blockchain: A Beginner's Guide to On-Chain Analysis

What is and How Does Blockchain Work?

Blockchain is a decentralized data distribution technology that allows for the creation of immutable and decentralized ledgers. It works based on a network of computers that work together to validate and record transactions in a secure and transparent manner.

How Does Blockchain Work?

Blockchain is composed of a series of blocks that contain information about transactions, such as the output address and the input address. Each block is identified by a unique number called a "hash," which is generated from the information contained in the block. When a transaction is recorded in the blockchain, it is verified by a network of computers that work together to ensure that the transaction is valid and has not been altered.

Types of Blockchain

There are two main types of blockchain: public and private.

Public Blockchain: A public blockchain is accessible to anyone and allows anyone to record transactions. Examples of public blockchain include the Bitcoin and Ethereum blockchains.

Private Blockchain: A private blockchain is controlled by a single entity and is not accessible to the public. Examples of private blockchain include a company's internal data management system.
