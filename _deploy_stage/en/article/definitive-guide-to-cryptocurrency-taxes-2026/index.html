What is a Cryptocurrency and How Does it Work

Cryptocurrencies are digital assets that use cryptography to secure and transfer value. They are based on blockchain technology, a decentralized ledger system that allows the creation and management of digital assets securely and transparently.

Types of Cryptocurrencies

There are several types of cryptocurrencies, including:

  • Cryptocurrencies: Digital assets that can be used as a medium of exchange, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum.
  • Tokens: Digital assets that represent a share of ownership in a company or project, such as tokens from an Initial Coin Offering (ICO).
  • NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens): Unique and non-interchangeable digital assets, such as digital art.

How the Tax Process Works

The tax process for cryptocurrencies involves several steps:

  1. Income Declaration: Cryptocurrencies are considered financial assets and must be declared in the income tax return.
  2. Income Tax: Cryptocurrencies are subject to income tax, which is calculated based on the market value of the asset.
  3. Financial Transaction Tax: Some countries also apply a financial transaction tax on the purchase and sale of cryptocurrencies.
