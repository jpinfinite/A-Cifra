The Revolution of Interest-Bearing [Stablecoins](/artigo/yield-bearing-stablecoins "Stablecoins que Pagam Juros: O Fim do Dinheiro Parado em 2026"): A New Chapter for Digital Economy

2025-2026: The Year of Transition to a No-End Economy

The 2020 decade was marked by a series of events that transformed the way people think about money and economy. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the adoption of digital technologies, including cryptocurrencies and mobile payment apps. The perception that physical money was an obstacle to mobility and economic efficiency began to change. In 2025, the digital economy is ready to take the next step: creating a no-end financial system.

The Pain of the Market: Idle Money and Inflation

One of the main problems of the traditional financial market is the existence of idle money. This occurs when money is deposited in bank accounts and not used for investments or consumption. According to estimates, about 70% of circulating money is idle in bank accounts. Additionally, inflation is a continuous problem, reducing the value of money over time. The combination of these two factors creates a situation where money is not effectively used to generate value.

How does it work "under the hood"?

Yield-bearing stablecoins work based on a combination of technologies, including:

* Reserve assets: Yield-bearing stablecoins maintain a reserve of assets in a reference currency, such as the US dollar. This ensures that the value of the stablecoin is maintained.
* Loans: Yield-bearing stablecoins lend part of their reserve assets to other investors, offering a yield.
* Interest rate: The interest rate is calculated based on the yield offered by the loans and is distributed to the holders of the stablecoin.
* Governance protocol: Yield-bearing stablecoins usually have a governance protocol that allows holders to vote on important decisions, such as the interest rate and reserve assets.
